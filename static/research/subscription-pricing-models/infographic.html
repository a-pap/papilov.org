04 — Psychology

Why We Keep Paying

Behavioral economics explains why subscription inertia is the most powerful force in the business.

🧠

Loss Aversion

Losing Netflix feels 2× worse than the rational savings of canceling. 42% of users delay cancellation even when dissatisfied.

2×
💤

Subscription Blindness

72% of consumers underestimate their total subscription spending by ~40%. 55% maintain at least one unused sub.

72%
🐸

Boiling Frog

Small, regular ~10% increases stay below the psychological pain threshold. Netflix: $1–2.50 every 12–24 months — too little to trigger action.

~10%
⚖️

Moral Fairness

Consumers judge prices morally, not economically. Netflix's 2011 60% hike felt like betrayal. The same increase spread over 5 years felt normal.

60% ≠ 5×12%
05 — Framework

Three Pricing Models

Every subscription falls into one of three strategic categories. The model determines whether prices go up, stay flat, or hide increases elsewhere.

Extractive

Subscription = the product. Raise as fast as churn allows.
Netflix Disney+ Spotify HBO Max
  • Revenue comes from subscriptions
  • Moderate switching costs
  • Ad tiers as pressure release
  • Churn elasticity ~1.8–5.5%

Gateway

Subscription = acquisition tool. Hold price, monetize elsewhere.
WoW Costco Planet Fitness Amazon Prime
  • Sub drives adjacent spending
  • Price = brand identity
  • Raise once per decade (if ever)
  • Prime members: $1,400/yr on Amazon

Monopoly

No real alternatives. Raise freely, cite AI/features.
Adobe CC Microsoft 365 Google Workspace
  • Extreme switching costs
  • Proprietary formats lock-in
  • AI features justify +30–43%
  • Minimal churn regardless
06 — Timeline

Pricing Disasters & Wins

2011
Netflix Qwikster — 60% price hike
800K subscribers lost. Stock dropped 77%. The case study in how NOT to raise prices: too much, too fast, no added value.
2017–2019
MoviePass — the opposite error
Slashed price from $30+/mo to $9.95 for daily movies. Lost $20M/month. 92.3% churn when forced to raise. Execs convicted of fraud.
2022 Q1
Netflix's first subscriber loss — the pivot
Wall Street shifted from subscriber growth to revenue per user. Every streamer began aggressive price increases simultaneously.
2022 May
EVE Online breaks 18-year freeze
+33% ($14.95 → $19.99). Player backlash was severe — reinforced WoW's decision to hold.
2023–24
Netflix ad-tier + password crackdown
Ad tier hit 94M users. Password crackdown added ~50M net subscribers. Revenue grew without proportional price pain.
2025 Jan
Netflix raises all tiers — no backlash
+$1–$2.50 across all plans. Reached 302M subscribers. Boiling frog strategy perfected over 14 years.
2025 Oct
Xbox Game Pass Ultimate — 50% single hike
$19.99 → $29.99 overnight. Cancellation website crashed. GameStop publicly mocked Microsoft.
