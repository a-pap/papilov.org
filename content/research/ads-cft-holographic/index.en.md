In 1997, Juan Maldacena published the most cited paper in the history of high-energy physics (over 20,000 citations). He demonstrated a precise mathematical correspondence between quantum gravity in a bulk spacetime and a quantum field theory living on its boundary — with no gravity and one fewer dimension.

This is the AdS/CFT correspondence: a concrete realization of the holographic principle that grew from black hole thermodynamics. The interactive explainer below traces the path from the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy formula (1973) to modern attempts to apply holography to our Universe.

## Key Ideas

Black hole entropy is proportional to the area of its event horizon, not its volume (Bekenstein, 1973; Hawking, 1975). This was the first indication that information about the contents of a region of space can be fully encoded on its boundary.

Maldacena (1997) turned this insight into a precise mathematical duality: string theory in 5-dimensional anti-de Sitter space is equivalent to a 4-dimensional conformal Yang-Mills theory on its boundary. Every object in one description has an exact counterpart in the other.

Ryu and Takayanagi (2006) showed that the entanglement entropy of a boundary region equals the area of a minimal surface in the bulk. Van Raamsdonk (2010) drew a radical conclusion: spacetime is "stitched together" by quantum entanglement. Remove entanglement — spacetime falls apart.

The problem: AdS/CFT works for spaces with negative cosmological constant. Our Universe has a positive one (de Sitter). Attempts to construct dS/CFT (Strominger, 2001) are actively developing, but a full holography for our Universe remains elusive.
